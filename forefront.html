Ramgarhia Sabha at the forefront of relief, calls on NGOs to unite for flood-hit Panjab

30 Aug 2025 | Updated 30 Aug 2025

After recent record floods in Punjab, Ramgarhia Sabha is providing help while urging other NGOs to collaborate and coordinate efforts to ensure help effectively reaches all affected families.

Once again Panjab is subject to record breaking floods that have submerged over 850 villages, thousands of families are experiencing loss of livelihood and livestock , and sadly an alarming number of fatalities. Everyone at Ramgarhia Sabha is deeply distressed at the current situation, but stands resolute to stand with Panjab in every way possible during this devastating time.

Ramgarhia Sabha, having worked in dozens of countries and countless humanitarian crisises, as well as three massive floods in Panjab in the last several years, has a wealth of experience, logistics and resources already being put to use on the ground since day one. However, experience from previous help missions tells us that it is common that resources do not always get distributed in a manner that provides for maximum coverage, with some pockets getting saturated with assistance while others are left in dire need.

At this time, taking inspiration from the villages and families that have come to stand together to face this catastrophic event, Ramgarhia Sabha is asking that all NGO's work collectively and open lines of communication to ensure that help makes it to every area and home in need.

As always, we welcome all NGOs to connect with Ramgarhia Sabha via email at Manvinder.ranauta@gmail.com to plan, collaborate, and deliver help strategically to ensure, that collectively, we reach as many impacted families as possible . Working together, in this initial phase and beyond, we can ensure that help is delivered systematically and as effectively as possible.
